Parameters - ToolTip:
Solar Radiation (MJ/m²) :

Solar radiation is the energy emitted by the Sun, including light, heat, and UV rays, which drives Earth's climate, supports life, and can be harnessed for renewable energy.

Infiltration rate (mm/h):

Infiltration rate is the speed at which water soaks into the ground, influencing soil moisture, groundwater recharge, and water runoff in an area.

Field Capacity (%):

Field capacity is the amount of water soil can hold after excess water has drained, representing the optimal moisture level for plant growth.

Wilting Point (%):

Wilting point is the soil moisture level at which plants can no longer extract water, causing them to wilt and potentially die.

Crop Coefficient (Kc):

Crop coefficient is a factor used to estimate the water needs of a specific crop, reflecting how much water it requires relative to reference crops under similar conditions.

Irrigation System Efficiency (%):

Irrigation system efficiency measures how effectively an irrigation system delivers water to crops, indicating the percentage of water that is used for plant growth versus being lost to evaporation or runoff.

Slope (%):

Slope refers to the steepness or incline of the land surface, which affects water drainage, erosion, and the suitability for agriculture or construction.
